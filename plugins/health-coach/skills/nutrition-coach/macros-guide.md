# Macros Guide — Mediterranean context

Rough reference values. These are approximations for coaching purposes, not precise nutrition labels.

## Portion estimation (without a scale)

- Palm of the hand ≈ 100-120 g of cooked protein (chicken, fish, tofu)
- Closed fist ≈ 1 cup of cooked grains or pasta (~150-200 g cooked)
- Cupped hand ≈ 30-40 g of nuts or cheese
- Thumb ≈ 1 tablespoon of oil or nut butter (~10-15 g)

## Typical Mediterranean meals — rough estimates

- **Potato omelet** (1/6 slice of a 4-egg, 2-potato omelet): ~200 kcal, 8 g protein, 15 g carbs, 12 g fat
- **Lentil stew with vegetables** (1 plate, ~300 g): ~350 kcal, 18 g protein, 45 g carbs, 8 g fat
- **Mixed salad with tuna** (standard): ~250 kcal, 20 g protein, 10 g carbs, 15 g fat
- **Ham sandwich** (baguette 80 g + 50 g cured ham): ~350 kcal, 20 g protein, 40 g carbs, 10 g fat
- **Seafood paella** (plate, ~300 g): ~450 kcal, 20 g protein, 60 g carbs, 12 g fat

## Rough daily references (NOT prescriptions — depend on the person)

These are **population averages** for moderately active adults. Do not give these as targets without a conversation about the person's goals.

- Protein: 1.2–1.8 g per kg of bodyweight for active adults
- Fiber: 25–35 g/day
- Water: 30–35 ml per kg bodyweight, more on training days

## How to think about "a good meal"

Rather than hitting exact numbers, aim for the plate model:
- ½ plate vegetables (any color, any prep)
- ¼ plate protein (animal or plant)
- ¼ plate carbs (preferably whole: legumes, whole grains, potato)
- A thumb of healthy fat (olive oil, nuts, avocado)

If a meal hits 3 of those 4 quadrants, it's a good meal. Don't chase perfection.
