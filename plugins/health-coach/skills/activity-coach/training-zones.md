# Training zones and RPE

## RPE scale (Rate of Perceived Exertion, 1-10)

Use this as the default — no device needed.

| RPE  | Feeling                                                       | Talk test                    |
| ---- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| 1-2  | Barely moving, warm-up pace                                   | Can sing                     |
| 3-4  | Easy, conversational                                          | Full sentences, easy         |
| 5-6  | Moderate, slightly out of breath                              | Short sentences              |
| 7    | Comfortably hard — sustainable for 30-60 min                  | Few words at a time          |
| 8    | Hard — sustainable for 10-20 min                              | Single words                 |
| 9    | Very hard — sustainable for a few minutes                     | Can't really talk            |
| 10   | Max effort, all-out                                           | No way                       |

## HR zones (5-zone model)

Requires a max HR estimate. Most accurate: a real max test. Rough estimate: `208 - 0.7 × age` (more accurate than 220-age for most adults).

| Zone | % of max HR | RPE equivalent | Purpose                               |
| ---- | ----------- | -------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Z1   | 50-60%      | 1-3            | Recovery, warm-up                     |
| Z2   | 60-70%      | 3-5            | Aerobic base — most training volume   |
| Z3   | 70-80%      | 5-7            | Tempo, "grey zone"                    |
| Z4   | 80-90%      | 7-9            | Threshold, hard sustained efforts     |
| Z5   | 90-100%     | 9-10           | Intervals, VO2 max                    |

## Intensity distribution — the 80/20 principle

For endurance training, most research supports:
- ~80% of total training time at easy intensity (Z1-Z2 / RPE 3-5)
- ~20% at moderate-to-hard (Z3-Z5 / RPE 6-9)

The most common mistake recreational athletes make is spending too much time at Z3 — not easy enough to recover, not hard enough to adapt. If weekly data shows most sessions hovering around 70-80% max HR, that's worth flagging.

## When zones and RPE disagree

Trust RPE on days when:
- Sleep was poor
- Temperature is extreme (heat jacks HR without matching effort)
- Early in a session before HR settles (first 5-10 min)
- After caffeine

A "normal-feeling" run with unusually high HR is often a sign of accumulated fatigue.
